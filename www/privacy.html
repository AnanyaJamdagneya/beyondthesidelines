Privacy Policy

Last updated June 15, 2026

Beyond the Sidelines helps you pair physical-therapy exercises with private journaling. This policy explains, plainly, what we collect and how we use it.

What we collect

  • Account info: your name and email (or, if you sign in with Google, the name and email Google shares with us).
  • Your recovery info: the injury and areas you choose, your limits, goals, and milestones.
  • Your activity: exercise progress and session reports, and your journal entries — mood, pain, notes, small wins, and any optional photos you add.

How we use it

Only to run the app for you: to save your progress, sync it across your devices, and show you your trends. We do not sell your data, and we never use it for advertising.

Where it’s stored

Your data is stored securely with Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore and Firebase Authentication) and encrypted in transit.

What your PT can and can’t see

If you connect to a physical-therapy center with a code, your therapist can see the exercises they assign you, your exercise progress and feedback, the pain rating and quick note you choose to send when you finish a session, and messages you send them. Your private journal — your daily mood, reflections, small wins, and photos — stays yours; your PT never sees it. You can disconnect from a center at any time.

Your choices

You can edit your information in the app anytime, turn off a PT connection once you’ve healed, and permanently erase your journal, progress, and goals with Profile → Delete my data.

Children

The app isn’t directed to children under 13, and we don’t knowingly collect their information.

Changes

If this policy changes, we’ll update this page and the date above.

Contact

Questions? Email us at support@beyond-the-sidelines.com.
